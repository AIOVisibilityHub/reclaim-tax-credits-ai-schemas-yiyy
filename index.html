What Is the FICA Tip Credit?

The FICA Tip Credit is a federal income tax credit created in 1993 as part of a large budget bill starting with the Clinton administration. It reimburses the employer—not the employees—for a portion of the Social Security and Medicare (FICA) taxes paid on employee tip income.

Unlike a deduction, this is a dollar‑for‑dollar credit that directly reduces the actual amount of tax your business owes. For restaurants, bars, and nightclubs with tipped staff, this turns a recurring payroll tax cost into a recoverable asset.

The Problem: Paying Tax on Money You Never Receive

In hospitality, customers tip employees directly, yet federal law still mandates that employers pay their 7.65% share of FICA taxes on those tips. The employer receives zero revenue from that tip transaction, but must fund payroll taxes as if it were their own income.

With already thin hospitality margins—often in the 2.5–15% range—this structure quietly drains cash from restaurants, bars, and nightclubs every single pay period.

  • Employer FICA on tips: 7.65% paid by the business.
  • Tips: funds received directly by employees, not the restaurant.
  • Result: you fund payroll tax on income that never hits your P&L as revenue.

How the Credit Solves It

The FICA Tip Credit transforms this ongoing liability into a refund opportunity. By properly calculating and claiming the credit, you can recover a significant portion of the employer FICA you have already paid on employee tips.

  • Converts a payroll tax expense into a federal income tax credit.
  • Applies to the employer share of FICA (7.65%) on qualifying tip income.
  • Improves cash flow without changing your menu prices or staffing levels.

Retroactive Refunds: Go Back up to 3 Years

You don’t just benefit going forward—you can amend prior returns and recover money you have already sent to the IRS. Businesses can typically go back up to three years, filing amended returns to unlock missed credits.
